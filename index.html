A Little About Me

Hi! Welcome to my first website! My name is Kristen, an elder millennial on an eternal journey of exploration and growth! Other than attempting basic html to build fan sites on Angelfire back when I was a pre-teen, I haven't had much experience with coding. But a couple of months ago, my fascination took hold and would not let go!
I'm a classic INTJ personality: analytical, inquisitive, logical, and goal-oriented. So when I began taking a closer look at software engineering, it felt like I had struck gold!

My goal is to develop my skills well enough to create (or contribute to) a new program for one or more of the following:

  • Online Fandom Creations (i.e. fan-fiction and art)
  • Online Teaching Resources (for teachers to create, share and/or sell lesson plans
  • Dental Office Software (after ten years working in dental administration, I know from experience how the current programs could benefit from a revamp!)
  • Easy DIY At-Home Karaoke
  • An App to help couples and/or friends decide: Where are we going to eat?


Of course, I expect (and hope) that as I continue to learn, things I never imagined may demand my attention.

Background of a Bieler

I have lived my entire life in the state of Texas, in several different cities! Ingleside, a smallish city along the gulf coast; Glen Rose, a quaint town famous for Dinosaur Valley State Park and Fossil Rim Wildlife Center; Stephenville, where I attended Tarleton State University; Austin, San Marcos, then finally my current home, Dallas, where I have resided for the past twelve years. My husband, Matt, and I are lucky enough to have a fantastic view from our apartment where we live with our 11 year old husky, Buckley, and 13 year old cat, Dora.

My favorite thing about living in Dallas is the vibrant art scene and plethora of Tex Mex restaurants!

I'm also fortunate enough to live within an easy distance to my parents (who still live in Glen Rose), as well as my sister and 7 year old niece, Emersyn, who is quite possibly the coolest girl I know.


Before deciding to shift my career into the tech world, I worked as an office manager for a dental practice (a field in which I worked for over ten years). I've also been an executive assistant, park ranger, park security and spent my earlier working years in the service industry (hosting/waiting tables). All of which taught me valuable lessons along the way!
